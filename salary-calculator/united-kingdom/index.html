How UK Income Tax Works (2026/27)

In the UK, income tax is calculated on earnings above the Personal Allowance of £12,570. The basic rate of 20% applies on income between £12,571 and £50,270. The higher rate of 40% applies from £50,271 to £125,140, and the additional rate of 45% applies above £125,140.

National Insurance (NI) is a separate deduction. For employees, Class 1 NI is charged at 8% on earnings between £12,570 and £50,270, and 2% above £50,270. NI goes toward the state pension and social security benefits.

How Much is £50,000 After Tax in the UK?

A UK employee earning £50,000 per year takes home approximately £39,520 per year (£3,293/month) after income tax and National Insurance. The effective combined rate is 21.0%.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much is £60,000 after tax in the UK? +
A UK employee earning £60,000 takes home approximately £45,357 per year (£3,780/month) after income tax (£11,432) and National Insurance (£3,211). The effective tax rate is 24.4%.
What is the UK Personal Allowance for 2026/27? +
The UK Personal Allowance is £12,570. This is the amount of income you can earn each year without paying income tax. The Personal Allowance is reduced by £1 for every £2 of income above £100,000.
How is National Insurance calculated in the UK? +
Class 1 National Insurance is charged at 8% on earnings between £12,570 and £50,270 per year, and 2% on earnings above £50,270. Employees earning below £12,570 pay no National Insurance.
What is the higher rate tax threshold in the UK? +
The higher rate (40%) applies to income between £50,271 and £125,140 for the 2026/27 tax year. Income above £125,140 is taxed at the additional rate of 45%.
