How Canadian Income Tax Works (2026)

Canadian federal income tax is progressive, starting at 15% on income above the Basic Personal Amount of C$15,705. The rates increase through five brackets up to 33% on income above C$246,752. Provinces also levy their own income tax, ranging from about 5% to 17.5%, which this page does not include.

The Canada Pension Plan (CPP) is deducted at 5.95% on earnings between C$3,500 and C$68,500. Employment Insurance (EI) is deducted at 1.64% on earnings up to C$63,200.

How Much is C$70,000 After Tax in Canada?

A Canadian employee earning C$70,000 per year takes home approximately C$56,952 per year (C$4,746/month) after federal income tax, CPP, and EI — before provincial tax. The effective federal rate is 18.6%.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much is C$100,000 after tax in Canada? +
After federal income tax, CPP, and EI, a C$100,000 salary nets approximately C$80,971 per year (C$6,748/month). Note this excludes provincial income tax, which varies by province.
What is the Basic Personal Amount in Canada for 2026? +
The federal Basic Personal Amount is C$15,705 for 2026. This is the amount of income you can earn without paying federal income tax.
What is CPP and how much do I pay? +
CPP (Canada Pension Plan) is deducted at 5.95% on employment income between C$3,500 and C$68,500, for a maximum annual contribution of C$3,867.50 in 2026.
What is EI in Canada? +
Employment Insurance (EI) premiums are deducted at 1.64% on insurable earnings up to C$63,200, for a maximum annual contribution of C$1,036.48 in 2026.
