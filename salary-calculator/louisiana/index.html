Louisiana Income Tax Overview (2026)

Louisiana reduced its top income tax rate to 3% in 2025 as part of a major tax reform. This flat 3% applies to all income. The reform also eliminated several deductions, simplifying the code.

In addition to state tax, all Louisiana workers pay federal income tax (10%–37% marginal rates) and FICA — Social Security (6.2%) and Medicare (1.45%), totaling 7.65% of gross wages. Voluntary deductions like 401(k) contributions or health insurance premiums further reduce taxable income.

Cost of Living in Louisiana

Louisiana has low housing costs. New Orleans median home prices are around $250,000, though insurance costs are high due to hurricane risk. Baton Rouge is more affordable. The cost of living is below national averages in most categories except insurance.

When evaluating a job offer in Louisiana, consider not just the salary but total compensation — employer benefits, retirement match, and location-specific costs. A $70,000 salary in a low cost-of-living Louisiana city may be more livable than $85,000 in a high-cost metro.

Top Industries and Employers in Louisiana

Louisiana's economy is anchored by oil and gas, petrochemicals, tourism, healthcare, agriculture. The state median household income is approximately $52,000 per year, though this varies significantly by region and occupation.

Louisiana follows the federal minimum wage floor of $7.25/hour, so entry-level pay here is set largely by employer competition rather than state law, and the state's $52,000 median household income sits close to the national average, with oil and gas as the largest single influence on local pay scales.

How Louisiana Compares to Other States

Louisiana's 3.0% top rate means a worker earning $75,000 pays approximately $2,250 in state income tax annually. Compared to zero-tax states, this reduces take-home by $2,250/year — a meaningful difference that compounds significantly over a career.

How to Calculate Your Louisiana Take-Home Pay

Your net pay is your gross salary minus: (1) federal income tax based on your bracket and filing status, (2) FICA at 7.65% of gross wages, (3) Louisiana state income tax at 3.0%, and (4) any voluntary deductions such as 401(k), HSA, or health insurance premiums. The calculator above handles steps 1–3 automatically.

Tax-Advantaged Strategies for Louisiana Workers

Regardless of your state tax rate, contributing to pre-tax retirement accounts like a 401(k) or traditional IRA reduces your federal taxable income dollar for dollar. In 2026, you can contribute up to $23,500 to a 401(k) and $7,000 to an IRA. For someone earning $60,000 in Louisiana, maxing the 401(k) reduces federal taxable income by $23,500 — saving approximately $2,820 in federal tax (at 12% marginal rate) plus approximately $705 in Louisiana state tax.
