Alabama Income Tax Overview (2026)

Alabama has a graduated income tax with rates of 2%, 4%, and 5% on income above $3,000 (single). The top 5% rate applies to income over $3,000 for single filers.

In addition to state tax, all Alabama workers pay federal income tax (10%–37% marginal rates) and FICA — Social Security (6.2%) and Medicare (1.45%), totaling 7.65% of gross wages. Voluntary deductions like 401(k) contributions or health insurance premiums further reduce taxable income.

Cost of Living in Alabama

Alabama has one of the lowest costs of living in the US. The median home price is around $210,000, and a one-bedroom apartment in Birmingham averages $950/month.

When evaluating a job offer in Alabama, consider not just the salary but total compensation — employer benefits, retirement match, and location-specific costs. A $70,000 salary in a low cost-of-living Alabama city may be more livable than $85,000 in a high-cost metro.

Top Industries and Employers in Alabama

Alabama's economy is anchored by automotive manufacturing, aerospace, healthcare, retail. The state median household income is approximately $52,000 per year, though this varies significantly by region and occupation.

Alabama follows the federal minimum wage floor of $7.25/hour, so entry-level pay here is set largely by employer competition rather than state law, and the state's $52,000 median household income sits close to the national average, with automotive manufacturing as the largest single influence on local pay scales.

How Alabama Compares to Other States

Alabama's 5.0% top rate means a worker earning $75,000 pays approximately $3,750 in state income tax annually. Compared to zero-tax states, this reduces take-home by $3,750/year — a meaningful difference that compounds significantly over a career.

How to Calculate Your Alabama Take-Home Pay

Your net pay is your gross salary minus: (1) federal income tax based on your bracket and filing status, (2) FICA at 7.65% of gross wages, (3) Alabama state income tax at 5.0%, and (4) any voluntary deductions such as 401(k), HSA, or health insurance premiums. The calculator above handles steps 1–3 automatically.

Tax-Advantaged Strategies for Alabama Workers

Regardless of your state tax rate, contributing to pre-tax retirement accounts like a 401(k) or traditional IRA reduces your federal taxable income dollar for dollar. In 2026, you can contribute up to $23,500 to a 401(k) and $7,000 to an IRA. For someone earning $60,000 in Alabama, maxing the 401(k) reduces federal taxable income by $23,500 — saving approximately $2,820 in federal tax (at 12% marginal rate) plus approximately $1,175 in Alabama state tax.
