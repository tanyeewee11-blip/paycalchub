How Australian Income Tax Works (2025–26)

Australian residents pay no income tax on the first A$18,200 of income (the tax-free threshold). Above this, the tax rate rises progressively from 19% through to 45% on income over A$180,000. A Medicare Levy of 2% applies to most taxpayers on top of income tax.

Superannuation is an important part of Australian employment — your employer is required to pay 11.5% of your ordinary earnings into a super fund. This is paid on top of your salary and is not deducted from your take-home pay.

How Much is A$80,000 After Tax in Australia?

An Australian employee earning A$80,000 per year takes home approximately A$61,933 per year (A$5,161/month) after income tax (A$16,467) and Medicare Levy (A$1,600). The effective tax rate is 22.6%.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much is A$100,000 after tax in Australia? +
An Australian employee earning A$100,000 takes home approximately A$75,033 per year (A$6,253/month) after income tax (A$22,967) and the 2% Medicare Levy (A$2,000). The effective rate is 25.0%.
What is the tax-free threshold in Australia? +
The tax-free threshold is A$18,200. Australian residents who earn less than this amount pay no income tax. You can claim the tax-free threshold from your employer by completing a Tax File Number (TFN) declaration.
What is the Medicare Levy in Australia? +
The Medicare Levy is 2% of your taxable income, paid by most Australian taxpayers to fund the public health system. Low-income earners may receive a reduction or exemption from the levy.
What is superannuation in Australia? +
Superannuation (super) is a compulsory retirement savings system. Your employer must pay 11.5% of your ordinary earnings into a super fund in 2025–26. This is paid in addition to your salary, not deducted from it.
