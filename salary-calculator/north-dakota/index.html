North Dakota Income Tax Overview (2026)

North Dakota has a state income tax (flat 2.5% rate). On top of federal income tax and FICA, this reduces take-home pay compared to no-income-tax states. The capital city is Bismarck; the largest city is Fargo.

On top of state tax, all North Dakota workers pay federal income tax (10%–37%) and FICA — Social Security (6.2%) and Medicare (1.45%), totaling 7.65% of gross pay.

A worker earning $60,000 in North Dakota pays approximately $1,500 in state income tax per year, on top of federal taxes.

How to Calculate Your North Dakota Take-Home Pay

Your net pay is gross salary minus: (1) federal income tax, (2) FICA (7.65%), (3) North Dakota state income tax (2.5%), and (4) any voluntary deductions like 401(k) or health insurance. Use our Salary Calculator to model all deductions precisely.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much is $50,000 after taxes in North Dakota? +
A single filer earning $50,000 in North Dakota takes home approximately $40,964 per year ($3,414/month) after federal income tax, FICA, and North Dakota state income tax.
Does North Dakota have a state income tax? +
Yes. North Dakota has a state income tax (flat 2.5% rate). This is deducted from your paycheck in addition to federal income tax and FICA.
What is the total effective tax rate in North Dakota on $75,000? +
For a single filer earning $75,000 in North Dakota, the combined effective rate is approximately 21.0%, leaving a take-home of $59,274 per year.
