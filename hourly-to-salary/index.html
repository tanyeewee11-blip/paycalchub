How to Convert Hourly Wage to Annual Salary

The standard formula is simple: hourly rate × hours per week × weeks per year. For a full-time employee working 40 hours per week across 52 weeks, that equals 2,080 working hours per year. A $25/hour wage therefore translates to $52,000 annually before taxes.

Part-time workers, contractors, and shift workers should adjust the hours per week and weeks per year fields above to reflect their actual schedule. If you take unpaid leave or work seasonally, selecting 48 or 50 weeks gives a more accurate picture of your true annual income.

After-Tax Hourly Equivalent

Your gross hourly rate and your after-tax hourly equivalent are meaningfully different. Federal income tax, FICA (7.65%), and state income tax all reduce what you actually take home per hour worked. At $25/hour ($52,000/year), a single filer in a no-state-tax state takes home approximately $43,200/year — roughly $20.77/hour net.

Overtime Pay

If you regularly work overtime, add those hours in the calculator above using the Overtime field. Under federal FLSA rules, hourly workers earn 1.5× their regular rate for all hours over 40 per week. Some states (California, Alaska) require overtime after 8 hours in a single day regardless of weekly total. See our Overtime Pay Guide for the full rules.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I convert hourly to annual salary? +
Multiply your hourly wage by hours per week, then by weeks per year. For a standard full-time job: hourly rate × 40 hours × 52 weeks = annual salary. A $25/hour rate equals $52,000 per year working full-time.
What does $15/hour equal annually? +
$15/hour × 40 hours × 52 weeks = $31,200 per year before taxes. After federal taxes and Social Security/Medicare, take-home pay would be approximately $25,000–$26,000 annually depending on your state.
How many working hours are in a year? +
A standard full-time schedule is 2,080 hours per year (40 hours × 52 weeks). After accounting for 10 federal holidays and 2 weeks vacation, a typical work year is closer to 1,960 hours. Most salary calculations use 2,080 as the standard denominator.
What is $20/hour as an annual salary? +
$20/hour × 2,080 hours = $41,600/year gross. After federal income tax and FICA, a single filer in a no-state-tax state takes home approximately $34,800/year ($2,900/month). In states with income tax, take-home is lower.
How much is $30/hour per year? +
$30/hour × 2,080 hours = $62,400/year gross — above the US individual median income. After taxes in a no-state-tax state, a single filer takes home approximately $50,800/year ($4,233/month).
Does overtime count toward my annual salary? +
Yes — if you regularly work overtime, add it using the Overtime field in the calculator above. Standard federal overtime is 1.5× your regular rate for hours over 40/week. Consistent overtime can add $5,000–$20,000+ to your annual income depending on your base rate and hours worked.
