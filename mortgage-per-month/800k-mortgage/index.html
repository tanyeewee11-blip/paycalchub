What Goes Into a $800,000 Monthly Mortgage Payment?

Your monthly payment on a $800,000 mortgage has several components. The principal and interest (P&I) payment at 6.9% on a 30-year loan is $5,269/month. But your actual monthly cost will be higher:

  • Principal & Interest: $5,269/month (at 6.9%, 30-year)
  • Property taxes: varies by location, typically $833–$1,667/month (1–2% of home value annually)
  • Homeowner's insurance: typically $333–$500/month
  • PMI (if less than 20% down): typically $333–$1,000/month until you reach 20% equity

Adding estimated taxes and insurance, the total monthly cost of a $800,000 mortgage (assuming 20% down on a $1,000,000 home) is approximately $6,519/month.

30-Year vs 15-Year: Which is Better for $800,000?

On a $800,000 mortgage at 6.9%, the monthly payment difference between a 30-year ($5,269/mo) and 15-year ($7,146/mo) is $1,877/month. However, the 15-year loan saves $610,560 in total interest over the life of the loan.

The 30-year makes sense if cash flow is a priority or you plan to invest the payment difference. The 15-year makes sense if you can afford the higher payment and want to build equity faster and pay less overall.

How Much Income Do You Need for a $800,000 Mortgage?

Using the 28% rule (housing costs should not exceed 28% of gross monthly income), you need approximately $279,386/year to comfortably afford a $800,000 mortgage including taxes and insurance ($6,519/month total). At the 36% rule, the minimum income is $217,300/year.

Most lenders allow a debt-to-income ratio up to 43–45% in practice, but staying closer to 28–36% gives you more financial breathing room. See our affordability guide for a full breakdown by salary.

How Rate Changes Affect Your $800,000 Payment

On a $800,000 loan, each 0.5% change in interest rate changes your monthly payment by approximately $270. A 1% increase from 6.9% to 7.9% would raise your monthly payment from $5,269 to $5,814, adding $545/month and $196,200 in total interest over 30 years.
