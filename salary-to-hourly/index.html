How to Convert Annual Salary to Hourly Rate

The formula is straightforward: annual salary ÷ 2,080 = hourly rate, where 2,080 is the number of hours in a standard full-time work year (40 hours/week × 52 weeks). A $70,000 salary divided by 2,080 equals $33.65/hour before taxes.

This calculation gives you your gross hourly rate — what you earn before federal income tax, FICA (Social Security and Medicare), and any state income tax. Your actual take-home hourly equivalent is lower. Use our Salary Calculator to see the after-tax figure for your specific state.

Why Knowing Your Hourly Rate Matters

Converting your salary to an hourly rate is useful in several real situations:

  • Comparing job offers: A salaried offer and a contractor rate are easier to compare when both are expressed as hourly rates — especially once you factor in benefits, overtime eligibility, and paid time off.
  • Freelance pricing: If you're moving from employment to consulting, knowing your effective hourly rate helps set a baseline. Freelancers typically need to charge 1.5–2× their salaried hourly equivalent to account for self-employment tax, benefits, and unpaid hours.
  • Evaluating overtime: Knowing your hourly rate makes it easy to calculate how much a 5- or 10-hour overtime week actually adds to your paycheck — and whether the extra hours are worth it after tax.
  • Salary negotiation: Employers sometimes offer salaries that sound large but translate to below-market hourly rates. Running the conversion keeps the comparison honest.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many hours are in a work year? +

A standard full-time work year is 2,080 hours (40 hours/week × 52 weeks). Excluding 10 federal holidays, it drops to 1,960 hours. Most salary-to-hourly conversions use 2,080 as the standard denominator.

Does my hourly rate change if I take vacation? +

For salaried employees, paid vacation doesn't change your hourly equivalent — you're paid the same regardless of time off. For hourly workers or contractors calculating an annual equivalent, use fewer weeks in the denominator if you take unpaid time off. 50 weeks instead of 52 gives a more accurate picture for workers who take 2 weeks unpaid.

What is $50,000 a year per hour? +

$50,000 ÷ 2,080 = $24.04/hour gross. After federal income tax and FICA, a single filer in a no-state-tax state takes home approximately $41,700/year ($20.05/hour net). See the full breakdown on our $50,000 salary guide.

How do I calculate my hourly rate after tax? +

Take your annual take-home pay (after all taxes) and divide by your total annual hours worked. For example: $49,000 take-home ÷ 2,080 hours = $23.56/hour net. Our Salary Calculator shows your exact after-tax annual pay by state, which you can then divide by your hours to get the net hourly rate.

Is a $30/hour job a good salary? +

$30/hour full-time equals $62,400/year — above the US individual median. It's a comfortable income in most mid-size US cities, livable with discipline in mid-tier metros, and tight in high-cost cities like San Francisco or NYC. See our good salary guide for a city-by-city breakdown.
