Is $40,000 a Year a Good Salary?

$40,000 a year is below average in most major US cities, though workable in rural or mid-size markets. It is below the US median household income but above the individual median in many states. After federal taxes, a single filer takes home approximately $34,178 per year — or $2,848 per month — to cover housing, food, transport, and savings.

At $40,000 a year, independent living is achievable in lower cost-of-living cities and rural areas. In major metros like New York or San Francisco, shared housing is typically necessary. Careful budgeting is essential to build savings at this income level.
