Is $50,000 a Year a Good Salary?

$50,000 a year is average — comfortable in most mid-size cities, tight in expensive metros. It sits near the US median individual income and is a common benchmark for entry-to-mid level professionals. After federal taxes, a single filer takes home approximately $42,214 per year — or $3,518 per month.

The honest answer depends entirely on where you live. In cities like Austin, Charlotte, or Columbus, $50,000 supports a solid single-adult lifestyle with room to save. In San Francisco, New York, or Boston, $50,000 is genuinely tight — housing alone can consume 50–60% of take-home pay. The state tax table above shows how dramatically purchasing power varies by location: a $50,000 salary in Texas leaves you with $4,650 more per year than the same salary in California.

Jobs That Pay Around $50,000 a Year

Common roles at this salary level include: elementary school teacher, dental assistant, IT support specialist, paralegal, police officer, medical coder, skilled tradesperson (HVAC, electrician's apprentice), and junior accountant. These are largely stable, in-demand occupations — many with defined benefit pensions or strong benefits that push total compensation well above the base salary.

For those looking to grow beyond $50,000: the most reliable paths are earning a relevant certification (AWS, CPA, PMP), moving into a supervisory role, or switching to a higher-demand sector within your current field. The jump from $50,000 to $65,000+ is achievable within 2–3 years in most of these fields.

How to Calculate $50,000/Year to Hourly

The standard formula: $50,000 ÷ 52 weeks ÷ 40 hours = $24.04/hr. If you work 50 weeks (taking 2 weeks unpaid leave), the equivalent hourly rate is $25.00. For overtime, the FLSA requires 1.5× pay beyond 40 hours/week for non-exempt workers — at $24.04/hr base, your overtime rate is $36.06/hr. To go the other direction — from hourly to annual salary — use our Hourly to Salary Calculator.

$50,000 vs the US Median

The Bureau of Labor Statistics reports a median hourly wage of approximately $23/hour for all US workers, and a median annual salary for full-time workers of around $59,000. At $50,000 a year, you are slightly above the median hourly rate but below the median for full-time annual earners. This makes $50,000 a solidly middle-income salary — not struggling, not comfortable everywhere, but a reasonable foundation to build from.

What is $50,000 a year after taxes? +
For a single filer in 2026, $50,000/year nets approximately $42,214 after federal income tax ($3,962) and FICA ($3,825) — about $3,518/month. State income tax reduces this further: from about $37,564 in California to the full $42,214 in zero-tax states like Texas or Florida.
