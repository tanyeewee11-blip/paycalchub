🇺🇸 Average American Salary 2026

Salary GuideFebruary 2026210 min read

The median individual American salary is approximately $59,000/year ($28.37/hour) in 2026, according to Bureau of Labor Statistics data. But averages hide enormous variation — a 22-year-old in Mississippi earns very differently than a 45-year-old software engineer in San Francisco. This guide breaks down US salary data by age, education, state, gender, and industry to help you benchmark your own earnings.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average American salary in 2026? +
The median individual US salary for full-time workers is approximately $59,000/year ($28.37/hour) in 2026. The mean (average) is higher at ~$71,000, pulled up by high earners. Median household income (all workers in a household combined) is approximately $80,610.
What is a good salary in the US in 2026? +
Any salary above $59,000/year puts you above the national individual median. A salary of $75,000–$100,000 is generally considered good for a single adult in most US markets — in the top 25–35% of individual earners. See our full guide on what is a good salary in 2026.
What percentage of Americans make over $100,000? +
Approximately 18–20% of US individual workers earn $100,000 or more annually. Among households (which may include two earners), about 34% have household incomes above $100,000.
How much does the average American make per hour? +
The median hourly wage for full-time US workers is approximately $28.37/hour in 2026 ($59,000/year ÷ 2,080 hours). The mean hourly wage is higher at ~$34.13/hour due to high earners pulling the average up.
