$28 an Hour is How Much a Year?

Salary Guide January 2026 8 min read

$28/hour × 2,080 hours = $58,240/year before taxes. After federal income tax and FICA, a single filer in a no-tax state takes home approximately $48,834/year ($4,070/month). This guide breaks down every pay period, after-tax pay by state, and a realistic monthly budget.

Is $28 an Hour a Good Wage?

At $28/hr, a single adult can live comfortably in most US cities and begin building savings and retirement contributions.

For context, the federal minimum wage is $7.25/hour ($58,240 annualized vs. $15,080 for minimum wage). The US median individual wage is approximately $28/hour ($59,000/year) as of 2026. At $28/hr, you are at the national median.

Frequently Asked Questions

$28 an hour is how much a year? +
$28 per hour equals $58,240 per year, assuming 40 hours per week and 52 weeks (2,080 hours). Working 50 weeks gives $56,000/year; part-time at 20 hours/week gives $29,120/year.
What is the take-home pay for $28 an hour? +
A single filer earning $28/hr takes home approximately $48,834/year or $4,070/month after federal income tax and FICA, in a state with no income tax. With state taxes, take-home is lower — typically $45,922–$48,834/year depending on your state.
Is $28 an hour a good wage? +
Yes — $28/hr is above the US median wage of ~$28/hour. At $28/hr, a single adult can live comfortably in most US cities and begin building savings and retirement contributions.
How much is $28 an hour monthly? +
At $28/hour, your gross monthly income is $4,853.33 ($$58,240/12). After federal taxes and FICA, take-home is approximately $4,070/month for a single filer with no state income tax.
