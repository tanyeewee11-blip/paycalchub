$27 an Hour is How Much a Year?

Salary GuideJune 20267 min read

$27/hour × 2,080 hours = $56,160/year before taxes. After federal income tax and FICA, a single filer in a no-state-tax state takes home approximately $46,100/year ($3,842/month). This guide covers every pay period, after-tax take-home by state, a realistic monthly budget, and how to move from $27/hr to $35+/hr.

Is $27 an Hour a Good Wage in 2026?

$27/hr ($56,160/year) is slightly above the US individual median income — comfortable in most mid-size markets, tight in high-cost cities. In practical terms:

  • Comfortable: Most Southern and Midwest markets — Memphis, Indianapolis, Oklahoma City, Omaha, El Paso. Independent living with savings is realistic.
  • Manageable: Mid-tier metros — Austin, Nashville, Phoenix, Denver. Renting is fine; buying requires planning.
  • Tight: High-cost metros — San Francisco, NYC, Seattle, Boston. Requires roommates or long commute for comfortable living.

How to Move from $27 to $35+/Hour

  • Targeted certification: Adding a high-value credential in your field typically moves $27/hr workers to $33–$40/hr within 12–18 months. CPhT → PharmD pathway, RN license for medical techs, or PMP for project management roles.
  • Employer switch: At $27/hr, switching employers yields 15–20% increases on average. The same role at a hospital system, tech company, or government agency often pays $32–$35/hr.
  • Overtime: At $27/hr, your overtime rate is $40.50/hr. Five overtime hours per week adds $10,530/year gross — a 19% income boost without changing jobs.
💡 Tax tip at $56k: At $56,160/year you're in the 22% federal bracket for the top portion of your income. Pre-tax 401(k) contributions save $0.22 per dollar contributed. Contributing $5,000 to a traditional 401(k) saves $1,100 in federal taxes immediately — and if your employer matches, it's the single highest-return financial move available.

✎ Editor's Note — June 2026
$27/hr sits right around the US individual median in 2026, which makes it the income point where the "is this enough?" question comes up most often. The honest answer: it depends almost entirely on geography. If you're at $27/hr and feeling financially squeezed in a mid-cost market, the issue may be fixable with a targeted credential or employer switch — the jump to $33–$35/hr is achievable within 12–18 months for most roles at this wage level.
