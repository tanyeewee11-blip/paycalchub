$33 an Hour is How Much a Year?

Salary GuideJune 20267 min read

$33/hour × 2,080 hours = $68,640/year before taxes. After federal income tax and FICA, a single filer in a no-state-tax state takes home approximately $55,500/year ($4,625/month). This guide covers every pay period, after-tax take-home by state, a realistic monthly budget, and the fastest paths from $33/hr to $45+/hr.

Is $33 an Hour a Good Wage in 2026?

$33/hr ($68,640/year) is comfortably above the US individual median — roughly top 35% of earners. In practical terms:

  • Comfortable: Most Midwest, Southern, and Mountain West markets. Homeownership is achievable with planning in most of these markets.
  • Livable with margin: Mid-tier metros like Denver, Austin, Nashville, Portland. Renting is fine; buying is within reach with a focused savings plan.
  • Tight: High-cost metros — San Francisco, NYC, Seattle, Boston. Doable but limited savings capacity solo.

Overtime at $33/Hour

At $33/hr, your overtime rate is $49.50/hour. Five overtime hours per week adds $12,870/year gross — pushing total annual income to $81,510.

How to Move from $33 to $45+/Hour

  • Specialty certification: Adding a board certification or advanced license typically adds $8–$15/hr at this income level. OCS for PTs, BCPR for OTs, master electrician license, or AWS/Azure for tech workers.
  • Management track: Moving to team lead or supervisor roles typically adds $5–$10/hr without requiring new external credentials.
  • Employer switch: At $33/hr, switching to a larger employer typically yields 15–20% increases. Target hospitals, tech companies, and government agencies where compensation is more formalized.
