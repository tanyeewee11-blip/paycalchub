🦷 Dental Hygienist Salary 2026

Career & Salary January 2026 9 min read

Dental hygienists earn a national median salary of $85,860/year ($41.28/hour) in 2026 — one of the highest pay levels for a 2-year associate degree profession in the US. With strong job security, flexible scheduling (many hygienists work part-time), and a positive work environment, dental hygiene consistently ranks among the best healthcare careers for work-life balance.

Job Outlook & Growth

The BLS projects dental hygienist employment to grow 9% from 2022–2032, faster than average. An aging population requiring more dental care, expanded dental insurance coverage, and growing awareness of the link between oral health and systemic health are all driving demand. Job prospects are strongest in underserved rural areas.

Education & Qualifications

Dental hygienists require an Associate of Applied Science in Dental Hygiene (2 years) from an accredited program, plus passing the NBDHE (National Board Dental Hygiene Examination) and a state clinical exam. A bachelor's or master's degree is required for roles in research, education, or public health. Continuing education is required to maintain licensure.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average dental hygienist salary in 2026? +
The national median dental hygienist salary is $85,860/year ($41.28/hour) in 2026. Entry-level hygienists earn ~$62,000, while experienced hygienists in high-paying states like Alaska and California earn $100,000–$116,000+.
Is dental hygiene a good career? +
Yes — dental hygiene offers above-median pay (often $80,000–$100,000+) achievable with just a 2-year associate degree, strong job security, flexible part-time options, and a positive working environment. It consistently ranks among the best-value healthcare careers.
Do dental hygienists make more than nurses? +
Dental hygienists (median $85,860) earn slightly more than LPNs ($54,000) but less than RNs ($81,220). However, dental hygienists typically require only a 2-year degree vs. 2–4 years for RN, making the pay-per-year-of-education ratio excellent.
Can dental hygienists make $100k? +
Yes — experienced dental hygienists in high-paying states (Alaska, California, Washington) or those working multiple offices or per diem shifts routinely earn $100,000–$120,000+/year.
✎ Editor's Note — June 2026
Dental hygiene continues to be one of the more stable and well-compensated two-year degree careers in 2026. Demand is structurally supported by an aging population and expansion of dental coverage under some state Medicaid programs. The salary ceiling has also moved up: hygienists in California, Washington, and New York metro areas are now regularly seeing $85–$100k+ in high-demand practices. One emerging trend: direct access hygienist models in states that allow independent practice are creating entrepreneurial income opportunities beyond the traditional dental office setting.
