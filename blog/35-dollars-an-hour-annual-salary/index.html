$35 an Hour is How Much a Year?

Salary Guide January 2026 8 min read

$35/hour × 2,080 hours = $72,800/year before taxes. After federal income tax and FICA, a single filer in a no-tax state takes home approximately $59,601/year ($4,967/month). This guide breaks down every pay period, what 401k and benefits quality mean at this income, and a realistic monthly budget.

Is $35 an Hour a Good Wage?

At $35/hr, you're earning above the US median. You can afford a solid lifestyle in most markets and accelerate savings goals.

For context, the federal minimum wage is $7.25/hour ($72,800 annualized vs. $15,080 for minimum wage). The US median individual wage is approximately $28/hour ($59,000/year) as of 2026. At $35/hr, you are above the national median.

$35/Hour: Above the National Median, Into Specialist Territory

$35/hr ($72,800/year) puts you meaningfully above the national median wage and typically requires either a specialized trade license, several years of management experience, or a technical associate's degree. This is the wage point where 401k and health insurance plan quality start to matter more to total compensation than the headline hourly rate — a $35/hr job with a poor 401k match can pay less in real terms than a $32/hr job with a strong match and HSA contribution.
