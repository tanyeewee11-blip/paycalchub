🗺️ Average Salary by State 2026 — All 50 States Ranked

Salary GuideJune 20269 min read

The national median annual wage for full-time US workers is approximately $60,580 in 2026 — but that number masks enormous variation between states. Massachusetts median wages are 63% higher than Mississippi's. More importantly, after factoring in state income taxes and cost of living, the ranking shifts significantly. This guide covers all 50 states by median wage, after-tax take-home, and real purchasing power.

Why the Gap Between States Is Widening

The salary gap between high-wage and low-wage states has grown since 2020, driven by three factors:

  • Remote work concentration: High-paying tech and finance jobs that previously required coastal location are now distributed more broadly — but the highest salaries still concentrate in coastal metros, pulling up state averages there.
  • Healthcare and government wages: States with large hospital systems and federal government employment (Maryland, Virginia, Massachusetts) see above-average wages from these sectors.
  • Minimum wage divergence: California and Washington minimum wages ($17–$18/hr) pull up the floor for low-wage workers in those states, raising the median even for service sectors.
💡 Remote work opportunity: If you earn a coastal salary ($80,000–$120,000) and can relocate to a lower-cost state, the purchasing power gain can be substantial. Moving from California to Texas on the same salary adds roughly $7,000–$12,000/year in after-tax take-home, while housing costs in comparable markets may be 30–50% lower.

Frequently Asked Questions

What state has the highest average salary in 2026? +
Massachusetts has the highest median annual wage at approximately $72,000, followed by Washington ($70,500) and Connecticut ($69,800). However, Massachusetts has a 5% income tax and high cost of living, so after-tax purchasing power is not necessarily the highest among these states.
What is the average US salary in 2026? +
The national median annual wage for full-time workers is approximately $60,580/year in 2026. The mean (average) is higher at approximately $65,400 due to the influence of high earners. Median is a better measure of what a typical worker earns — half earn more, half earn less.
Which state has the best salary after taxes and cost of living? +
Texas and Tennessee consistently rank among the best when combining no income tax with lower-than-average cost of living. Washington has no income tax but Seattle's high housing costs reduce the advantage for workers living there. Indiana and Ohio are underrated — low tax rates, reasonable wages, and very low housing costs create strong purchasing power.
What state has the lowest average salary? +
Mississippi has the lowest median annual wage at approximately $44,100, followed by West Virginia ($45,200) and Arkansas ($46,800). Importantly, cost of living in these states is also significantly lower — a $44,000 salary in Mississippi can afford a comfortable lifestyle comparable to $65,000+ in a high-cost state.
How does my salary compare to the average in my state? +

✎ Editor's Note — June 2026
The state salary rankings are useful but incomplete without cost-of-living context. Massachusetts leads in gross median wages, but a $72,000 salary in Boston — after taxes and housing — leaves less discretionary income than a $55,000 salary in Indianapolis. The more useful question is: what does your after-tax income buy in your specific local market? The gap between states in purchasing power terms is smaller than the gross wage gap suggests, and in some cases inverts entirely. One trend worth watching: smaller metros in traditionally low-wage states (Huntsville AL, Boise ID, Greenville SC) are seeing above-average wage growth driven by manufacturing and technology investment, quietly improving their position in these rankings year over year.
