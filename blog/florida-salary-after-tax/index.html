Florida Salary After Tax 2026

State Tax Guide January 2026 8 min read

Florida workers face no state income tax — only federal income tax and FICA apply to your paycheck. This guide shows exactly how much you take home at every income level in Florida, compares cost of living across major cities, and explains what makes Florida a tax-friendly state for employees.

Take-home pay only tells half the story. Here is what your dollars actually buy in Florida:

Tax Strategies for Florida Workers

Regardless of Florida's tax rules, there are key strategies to maximize your take-home pay and reduce your overall tax burden:

  • Maximize federal pre-tax accounts — no state deduction needed: Florida's no-tax status means 401(k) and HSA contributions only save federal tax and FICA. Still worth maxing — federal savings of 10–22%+ on every pre-tax dollar are substantial.
  • Budget carefully for insurance costs: Florida homeowner's insurance has risen sharply due to hurricane risk — average premiums are $3,600–$6,000+/year, 3–4× the national average. Factor this into total housing cost calculations.
  • Leverage no state capital gains tax: Florida has no state capital gains tax. Selling investments, real estate, or a business in Florida only triggers federal capital gains tax (0–20% + 3.8% NIIT) — a significant advantage over high-tax states.
  • Use 529 plans for college savings: Florida's 529 plan (Florida Prepaid) is well-regarded, though contributions aren't state-tax-deductible (no state tax to deduct from). Earnings grow and withdraw federally tax-free for qualified education expenses.
  • Watch Miami and South Florida cost of living: Miami's cost of living rivals many Northern metros. Rent, insurance, and dining costs have risen significantly since 2020. Factor actual cost of living into salary comparisons with higher-tax states.
