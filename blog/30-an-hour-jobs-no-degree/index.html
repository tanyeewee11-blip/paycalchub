Jobs That Pay $30 an Hour Without a Degree

Career & Pay June 2026 8 min read

$30/hour works out to $62,400/year before tax — above the median individual income in most US states. You don't need a four-year degree to get there. Skilled trades, transportation, and technical certifications all offer realistic paths to $30/hour or more.

Skilled Trades

Trades typically require an apprenticeship or trade school (often 1–4 years, frequently paid while you train) instead of a four-year degree, and many reach $30/hour well before reaching full journeyman status.

  • Electrician: Median pay is well above $30/hour for licensed journeymen; apprentices often clear $30/hour within a few years.
  • Plumber: Similar apprenticeship path; experienced plumbers in many markets earn $35–$50/hour.
  • HVAC technician: Certification-based (EPA 608, etc.), typically 6 months–2 years of training; $30/hour is common for technicians with 2–3 years of experience.
  • Elevator mechanic: One of the highest-paying trades; apprenticeship-based, often exceeding $40/hour at journeyman level.
  • Wind turbine technician: Typically a 1–2 year certificate program; median pay is consistently above $30/hour nationally.

Transportation & Logistics

  • Commercial truck driver (CDL): A CDL takes weeks, not years, to obtain. Long-haul and specialized freight (tanker, hazmat) routinely pay $30/hour-equivalent or more once experienced.
  • Heavy equipment operator: Crane, excavator, and other heavy equipment operators on union or large commercial job sites frequently earn $30+/hour.

Technical & Certification-Based Roles

  • IT support specialist (senior/Tier 2-3): Entry-level IT support starts lower, but technicians with CompTIA/Cisco certifications and a few years of experience commonly reach $30/hour.
  • Dental hygienist: Requires an associate degree (not a four-year bachelor's) and routinely pays well above $30/hour — among the highest-paying associate-degree careers.
  • Radiology/medical imaging technician: Associate-degree certification path; median pay is typically above $30/hour with a few years of experience.
  • Web developer / no-code specialist: Increasingly accessible through bootcamps and portfolios rather than a CS degree; freelance and contract rates often exceed $30/hour.
Note: Several roles above (dental hygienist, radiology tech) require an associate degree or postsecondary certificate — not a bachelor's degree. "No degree" in this context means no four-year degree, which is the most common bottleneck people are trying to avoid.

How to Get There Faster

  • Apprenticeships pay you to train — unlike a four-year degree, most trade apprenticeships pay a wage (often $18–$22/hour) starting day one, with built-in raises as you progress toward $30+/hour.
  • Stack certifications, don't just take the first job — an entry-level HVAC or IT job pays less initially, but each additional certification (EPA 608, CompTIA, etc.) tends to come with a real raise.
  • Union jobs often have published, transparent pay scales — worth checking local union halls for electricians, plumbers, and operating engineers, since wage progression is usually predictable and contractually guaranteed.

Frequently Asked Questions
