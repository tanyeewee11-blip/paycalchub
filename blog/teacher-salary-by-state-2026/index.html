🏫 Public School Teacher Salary 2026

Career & Salary January 2026 9 min read

Public school teachers earn a national median salary of $68,430/year ($32.90/hour) in 2026. But teacher pay varies more by state than almost any other profession — from $47,000 in Mississippi to $92,000 in California. This guide covers median teacher pay by state, experience level, grade level, and the real after-tax take-home picture.

Job Outlook & Growth

The BLS projects K-12 teacher employment to grow 4–5% from 2022–2032. Teacher shortages are significant in math, science, special education, and bilingual education — areas where candidates can negotiate higher starting salaries and signing bonuses. Many districts are also offering loan forgiveness and housing assistance to attract teachers.

Education & Qualifications

K-12 public school teachers require a bachelor's degree and state teaching license/certification. Many states require or incentivize a Master's degree (often adding $3,000–$8,000/year on the salary scale). Teachers also qualify for Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF) after 10 years of payments — worth tens of thousands of dollars in student loan relief.

Teacher Salary vs. Total Compensation

Teacher base salaries often understate total compensation. Key benefits include:

  • Pension: Most public school teachers receive a defined-benefit pension — often worth $500,000–$1,500,000 in lifetime value for a full-career teacher.
  • Health insurance: Employer-paid health coverage typically valued at $10,000–$20,000/year.
  • Summers off: 10–12 weeks of paid time off versus 2–3 weeks in the private sector.
  • Job security: Tenure after 3–5 years provides strong employment protections.
  • Loan forgiveness: PSLF forgives remaining federal student loans after 10 years — potentially $50,000–$150,000 in relief.
Total compensation context: A teacher earning $65,000/year with a pension, full health coverage, and summers off may have equivalent total compensation to a private-sector worker earning $90,000–$100,000.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average teacher salary in 2026? +
The national median public school teacher salary is $68,430/year in 2026. Pay ranges from $47,000 in the lowest-paying states (Mississippi, South Dakota) to $92,000 in California and $88,000 in New York.
Which state pays teachers the most? +
California pays the highest teacher salaries at a median of ~$92,000/year, followed by New York ($88k), Massachusetts ($85k), and Connecticut ($83k). However, cost of living in these states is also higher.
Do teachers get paid in the summer? +
Most public school teachers receive their annual salary spread over 12 monthly payments, so they receive paychecks year-round including summer. However, the school calendar (180 days) means teachers work 10 months and have summers free — a significant quality-of-life benefit.
Is teaching a good career financially? +
Teaching offers below-median base pay compared to other degree-required professions, but total compensation (pension, health insurance, summers, loan forgiveness, job security) is often undervalued. In high-paying states like CA and NY, senior teachers can earn $80,000–$105,000+.
✎ Editor's Note — June 2026
Teacher salaries have increased in most states since 2022 — a combination of political pressure, COVID relief funds, and genuine shortage-driven wage increases — but purchasing power has not recovered in many districts once inflation is factored in. The gap between states remains stark: Mississippi's average teacher salary is roughly $48,000; California's is $95,000+. What the averages miss: many high-paying states also have significantly higher costs of living, and California teachers face some of the highest housing costs in the country. The best combination of salary-to-cost-of-living for teachers in 2026 tends to be Texas (no income tax), Georgia, and parts of the Mountain West.
